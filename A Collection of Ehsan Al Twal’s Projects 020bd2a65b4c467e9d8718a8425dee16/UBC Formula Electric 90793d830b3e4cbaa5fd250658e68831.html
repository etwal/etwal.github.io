UBC Formula Electric is a student design team where we build a formula electric race car and compete in FSAE.

I am on the Hardware Sub-team and my tasks are mainly PCB design and Circuit debugging:

PCB Designed

Monolithic Module (4 Layers):

This PCB is used for:

  • The balancing of the batteries in the accumulator pack
  • Monitoring the temperature and voltage levels of the batteries in the battery pack

Assembled board:

3D top view

  • Layer 1
  • Layer 1 (shelved polygons)
  • Layer 2
  • Layer 3
  • Layer 4
  • Debugging the Module by attaching it to 16 cells to replicate a segment in the battery pack and connecting the board to the BMS core to

The module would be placed on top of a battery inside the accumulator pack, and screws would be drilled through the metal oval holes and into the battery and used to monitor the state of charge and temperature.

This board uses an LTC6813 microprocessor and isoSPI communication to send information to the main microprocessor of the car
